The Hogwarts Project

Our mission is to build the first full scale Hogwarts Castle in the Scottish Highlands by the year 2024

Stay Updated Learn More

Why build Hogwarts?

Ever since the first book came out in 1997, people of all ages have been enthralled with anything and everything Harry Potter. And after the movies started coming out, we fell in love with the castle that became the home of our favorite characters and the backdrop of our favorite scenes.


But what would it be like to actually live at Hogwarts? That's a question that many fans have asked themselves over the years. There have been attempts at recreating some of the magic, such as The Wizarding World of Harry Potter, which opened in Orlando in 2010. But that's only getting a taste of the experience. Instead of coming as a tourist for one day, imagine flying out to Scotland, taking the Hogwarts Express to Hogwarts, and spending a week in the mountains doing classes, activities, House Competitions, Common Room game nights, and preparing for your big Quidditch match. That's what we're trying to recreate. And we're fully committed to making it happen.

Location

We're building on Lake Shiel, a freshwater loch that served as the inspiriation for Hogwarts in the moive, which was digitally layed onto these mountains 

Activites

Castle activites include kayaking, paddleboarding, indoor swimming pool, indoor basketball court, and card games and movies for the common rooms

Capacity

Hogwarts will house 600 students per week. Invitations  will be given on a lottery basis

Quidditch

Hogwarts Castle will be built alongside Quidditch stadium in a new sports league, with weekly house semi-finals and finals games
